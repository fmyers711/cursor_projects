Detail Contract proposal plan — Phases 1–4

What each phase produces for the Apr–Jul ’26 proposal and the three-year contract.

Phase 1 — Discovery & baseline

Map today’s Splunk footprint and maturity.

  • Interviews: Swim-lane leads (e.g., Costanza, Gottor, Niles) — pain points and priorities.
  • Audit: ES coverage gaps; ITSI/APM, IT & DC Ops.
  • Metrics: Ingestion (GB/day), data retention requirements, search concurrency, license use — by group.

Phase 2 — Requirements & mapping

Tie Splunk scope to each department’s goals.

  • CyberSecurity: ES → ES Premier; hunting & compliance data sources.
  • IT Ops: ITSI/APM production readiness; Digital Resiliency KPIs.
  • DC Ops: Infra monitoring for capacity and automated IR.
  • AI: Splunk’s role in NYPA’s overall AI strategy.

Phase 3 — Sizing & workload

Size data and compute for a three-year view.

  • Ingestion: Growth (~15–20% YoY where planned); hot / warm / cold tiers.
  • Workload: ES Premier analytics load; ITSI + APM at full deployment.
  • Check: Sizing calculator vs. projections to hit performance SLAs.

Phase 4 — Proposal package

Single deliverable by Jul ’26: contract-ready plan and numbers.

  • Roadmap: ES Premier rollout; ITSI/APM go-live.
  • TCO: Three years — licenses, support, implementation PS.
  • Executive summary: Security & resiliency story (incl. Cisco alignment as needed).

Phase 2 Requirements & mapping — swim lane details

Phase 2 translates discovery into scoped initiatives: each swim lane defines what must be true in the target Splunk program (data, apps, ownership, and success measures). The items below expand the Phase 2 card in the proposal plan and are the inputs to sizing (Phase 3) and the commercial proposal (Phase 4).

Swim lane 1 — CyberSecurity

This lane sets the security analytics and operations runway: from today’s Enterprise Security (ES) footprint to ES Premier, with explicit priorities for hunting, compliance evidence, and SOC workflow.

  • Current → target: Document which ES domains, use cases, and data models are in use today vs. required for ES Premier; note gaps in detection logic, correlation, and analyst workflows.
  • High-value data: Onboard or prioritize sources that support advanced threat hunting (endpoint, network, identity, cloud) and compliance automation (audit-ready views, scheduled attestations, data retention requirements aligned to policy).
  • Risk & ownership: Align on who owns content lifecycle, tuning, and escalation paths so the proposal includes the right PS, training, and license scope.

Primary deliverable: Security Posture Improvement Plan — prioritized initiatives, data dependencies, and ES Premier transition path for the three-year contract.

Swim lane 2 — IT Operations

This lane defines how ITSI and APM are scoped and operated in production and how leadership defines Digital Resiliency in measurable terms—so Splunk becomes the system of record for service health, not only ad hoc dashboards.

  • Current → target: Document which ITSI/APM entities, KPIs, alert thresholds, and integrations are in use today vs. required for production; note gaps in runbooks, ownership, and day-two operations.
  • KPIs & services: Agree on service definitions, dependencies, and KPIs (e.g., availability, incident/breach of SLA, critical business transactions) that roll up to “Digital Resiliency” narratives for executives.
  • Consumption model: Clarify role-based views (NOC, app owners, leadership) and refresh cadence so Phase 3 can size search and concurrent usage realistically.

Primary deliverable: Digital Resiliency dashboarding — KPI catalog, ITSI/APM production scope, and dashboard/alert blueprint referenced in the contract.

Swim lane 3 — DC Ops

This lane covers infrastructure and facilities-aligned monitoring: capacity, faults, and change-related signals that feed planning cycles and, where appropriate, automated incident response integrated with IT workflows.

  • Capacity & planning: Metrics and trends that support forecasting (compute, storage, network, environmental where applicable) and tie to Splunk ingestion and data retention requirements.
  • Availability & events: Standardized views for uptime and degradation, correlated with application and dependency layers so root-cause and customer impact are visible in one place.
  • Automation scope: Which alerts trigger tickets, enrichment, or orchestration; guardrails and change windows so automated responses stay safe and auditable.

Primary deliverable: Capacity & availability reporting — scope of infra monitoring, reporting cadence, and automation boundaries for the proposal.

Swim lane 4 — Splunk & NYPA AI strategy

This lane connects Splunk to NYPA’s enterprise AI strategy: how operational and security data, search, and workflow in Splunk support trusted analytics, monitoring, and governance for AI initiatives—aligned to approved tools, data paths, and compliance expectations.

  • Current → target: Document how Splunk data, apps, and integrations show up in NYPA’s AI roadmap today vs. the desired state (e.g., lineage into analytics platforms, observability for AI services, audit evidence for model-related activity); note gaps in access control, retention, and operational ownership.
  • Use cases & guardrails: Prioritize Splunk-backed scenarios (resilience and security around AI workloads, usage/cost visibility, evidence for policy) and utilize Splunk as the foundational and analytics layer for machine data (MELT).
  • Stakeholders & narrative: Clarify who owns Splunk’s role in the AI story (IT, Cyber, data/office of the CIO) so the proposal can scope licenses, integrations, and PS consistently with NYPA’s AI priorities.

Primary deliverable: Splunk–AI alignment brief — scoped use cases, governance touchpoints, and proposal hooks that tie Splunk to NYPA’s AI strategy for the three-year contract.
