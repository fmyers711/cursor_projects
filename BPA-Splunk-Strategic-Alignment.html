Executive Summary

Bonneville Power Administration markets one-third of the Pacific Northwest's electrical power across more than 15,000 miles of high-voltage transmission lines. As BPA navigates grid modernization, escalating cyber threats, and stringent NERC CIP mandates, the convergence of IT and Operational Technology (OT) data has become mission-critical. Splunk serves as the foundational analytics platform that connects BPA's strategic priorities to measurable, outcome-driven results—transforming fragmented, reactive monitoring into proactive, resilient grid operations.

🛠️ How Splunk Supports BPA's Mission
🖥️

Unified IT / OT Visibility

Splunk ingests data from enterprise IT systems alongside OT environments—SCADA, PLCs, and remote terminal units—providing a single pane of glass for the CIO, CTO, and CISO. Correlating these data streams allows grid operators to determine whether an anomaly is a cyberattack, equipment degradation, or weather event in minutes rather than hours.

📑

Automated NERC CIP Compliance

Splunk automates collection, monitoring, and reporting of compliance data across the full infrastructure—from enterprise systems to remote substations. Immutable, automated audit trails replace error-prone manual processes, providing continuous audit readiness and freeing thousands of engineering hours for strategic initiatives.

🔮

Predictive Grid Resilience

Advanced analytics applied to historical and real-time machine data identify failure patterns before they escalate. BPA shifts from costly scheduled maintenance to targeted predictive maintenance—extending asset lifespan, reducing service disruptions, and improving overall grid reliability during extreme weather and high-demand periods.

🚧 Barriers Overcome with Splunk

Challenge: Data Fragmentation

Grid operators and security teams rely on siloed tools. When an anomaly occurs, determining root cause—mechanical failure, weather event, or cyber intrusion—takes hours due to lack of unified data context across IT and OT environments.

Splunk Solution: Single Source of Truth

Splunk creates a unified data layer ingesting from all sources—network logs, SCADA telemetry, endpoint data, and weather feeds—enabling cross-domain correlation in real time. Operators gain full situational awareness from one interface.

Challenge: Manual Compliance Burden

Proving NERC CIP compliance is a highly manual, resource-intensive process. Auditing access logs and configurations across thousands of remote substations drains engineering resources and introduces human error risk.

Splunk Solution: Continuous Compliance Automation

Splunk continuously collects and indexes compliance evidence, auto-generates audit-ready reports, and alerts on policy deviations in real time. Engineering teams regain up to 80% of audit-related time for higher-value work.

Challenge: Alert Fatigue & Detection Gaps

As BPA adds sensors across the grid, data volume overwhelms existing infrastructure. Without automated correlation, critical security and grid stability signals are buried in noise, increasing response latency and escalation risk.

Splunk Solution: Intelligent Correlation & Alerting

Splunk's machine learning–driven analytics suppress low-fidelity noise and surface high-priority events automatically. Security teams act on a curated signal stream rather than raw alert volumes, dramatically reducing MTTR.
