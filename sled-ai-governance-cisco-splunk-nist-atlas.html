Executive summary

AI—and increasingly autonomous agentic AI and the Model Context Protocol (MCP)—now drives mission-critical decisions across state government and higher education: benefits eligibility, fraud detection, admissions, research workflows, and student services. These systems introduce risks traditional governance cannot handle and a rapidly expanding adversarial attack surface.

Most organizations have policies but lack the visibility, security, monitoring, and evidence to manage AI risk at scale—or defend against real adversary techniques. Cisco and Splunk provide an integrated stack designed to close those gaps for statewide and system-wide AI programs.

The governance challenge for SLED AI adoption

Why statewide and system-wide AI is hard to govern

  • Decentralized adoption — agencies, campuses, and research units deploy AI independently, creating shadow AI and inconsistent controls.
  • Mandate pressure without tooling — executive orders, board policies, and NIST-aligned requirements demand evidence most organizations cannot produce.
  • Agentic AI expands the attack surface — data/model poisoning, prompt injection, jailbreaks, supply-chain compromise, agent context/RAG poisoning, credential harvesting, data exfiltration, and abuse of non-human identities (NHIs).
  • Compliance constraints — CJIS, FERPA, HIPAA, and state privacy laws require data residency, audit trails, and controlled inference environments.
  • Identity blind spots — AI agents, MCP servers, and API keys operate as identities few organizations inventory or govern.

Barriers leadership must address

  • No unified inventory of AI systems, models, agents, or NHIs across the enterprise
  • No continuous evaluation of AI output quality, safety, bias, or drift in production
  • No runtime protection against prompt injection, tool misuse, or agent poisoning
  • No supply-chain scanning for poisoned models, datasets, or MCP tool repositories
  • No correlation between AI security events and broader SOC incident response
  • No audit-ready evidence package tying controls to framework subcategories

Discovery questions for leadership conversations

Governance & risk (NIST AI RMF)

  • Do you have a statewide or system-wide inventory of AI systems, models, agents, and NHIs?
  • Can you produce audit-ready evidence mapping controls to NIST AI RMF subcategories?
  • How do you evaluate AI output quality, bias, and drift in production—not just at deployment?
  • What is your process for safe decommissioning of AI systems and retiring agent identities?

Threat defense (MITRE ATLAS)

  • Resource Development: Do you scan for poisoned models, datasets, and agent tools before production?
  • Persistence: Can you detect agent context, RAG, and tool-data poisoning at runtime?
  • Credential Access: Do you monitor AI-agent credential harvesting and secret exposure?
  • Defense Evasion: Is AI-related activity correlated for incident response across the SOC?
  • Exfiltration: Can you prevent LLM data leakage and exfiltration via agent tool invocation?
  • AI Attack Staging: Do you red-team models against adversarial data and proxy-model attacks?
