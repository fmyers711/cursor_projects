Splunk for quantum computing in higher education

Consolidated best-fit use cases for security, availability, and resource optimization — with the most defensible metrics

Synthesized from three analyses (ChatGPT 5.4, Claude Haiku 4.5, Gemini 3.1), April 2026. Folder: Engagements Internal / Quantum Computing / Summary analysis.

Executive consensus

Across the three documents, roughly 70–82% of mature HPC-oriented Splunk patterns apply to quantum computing systems (QCS) with low-to-moderate customization on the classical, scheduling, identity, and governance layers. The weakest early fit is undifferentiated anomaly scoring at the raw device-physics layer without calibration context.

Most defensible positioning: Splunk as the unified correlation and observability layer for the whole quantum platform — identity, orchestration, schedulers, classical control plane, compliance, utilization, and (as telemetry matures) calibration, facility, and hybrid workflow IDs — not solely as a log sink for the QPU.

1. Security — best-fit use cases

All three analyses rank identity, privileged access, and cross-domain SIEM correlation as Tier 1: direct HPC carryover with high audit value for IP, collaborators, and vendor access.

Core Identity & privileged access

  • SSO/MFA, VPN, bastions, JupyterHub, APIs, vendor/support portals
  • Controller and admin-plane access; role-based job submission
  • Cloud provider API keys (IBM, Rigetti, Quantinuum, etc.) where used

Core Security event correlation

  • Correlate IAM, endpoint, network, scheduler, and controller events
  • Patterns: privilege escalation → data movement; vendor login → config/firmware change
  • Insider and collaborator risk (stale accounts, unusual egress)

Extended Compliance & audit

  • Attribution for experiments; admin changes; export-control awareness where applicable
  • Sponsor-ready trails (NSF, DOE, institutional policy)

2. Availability — best-fit use cases

Availability is defended through scheduler health, classical control-plane SLOs, and end-to-end hybrid workflow latency — not abstract “quantum uptime” alone.

Core Scheduler & job lifecycle

  • Queue depth, median wait time, success/failure rates, retries, throughput
  • Reservation efficiency (booked vs. used QPU time)
  • Transpilation time and failure rate (workflow friction before hardware)

Core Classical infrastructure

  • Orchestration servers, storage, APIs, containers/Kubernetes if present
  • Controller errors, firmware events, orchestrator–controller latency
  • Service availability, MTTR, downtime impact on funded work

Phase 2+ Quantum-aware & facility

  • Calibration age vs. job outcomes; fidelity/readout trends (baselines per calibration window)
  • Cryogenics, vibration, power, RF correlated with errors or failures
  • Workflow IDs across notebook → transpile → execute → retrieve

3. Resource optimization — best-fit use cases

Scarce QPU time makes utilization, chargeback, and capacity signals the strongest business case; all three documents align on governance and funding justification.

  • Attribution: QPU runtime and shots by PI, lab, project, department, funding source
  • Efficiency: Cost per completed experiment; reservation utilization; queue vs. demand
  • Hybrid economics: On-prem vs. cloud cost per comparable experiment (where measurable)
  • Research productivity: Stage-level failure rates (classical vs. QPU vs. retrieval) to target improvements

Most defensible metrics (consolidated)

“Defensible” = objectively measurable from logs/APIs/telemetry, explainable to IT/security/research leadership, actionable, and stable enough to trend. Thresholds below are examples from the source analyses — tune to your environment.

Tier 1 — deploy first

Theme Metric Why it holds up
Security Failed authentication attempts (e.g., per user/hour) Standard across SSO, VPN, bastions, notebooks; brute-force and misconfiguration signal.
Security Successful privileged logins & admin actions Audit-critical; ties to controllers, schedulers, firmware.
Security Vendor/support session activity Third-party risk; time-bounded maintenance windows.
Security Stale/disabled accounts with activity; unusual egress Higher-ed collaborator churn; IP and data-loss scenarios.
Operations Queue depth; median wait time Robust vs. mean for skewed queues; fairness and saturation.
Operations Job success rate; failure rate; retry frequency Direct reliability and UX indicators.
Operations Scheduler throughput; reservation utilization Capacity and booking discipline on expensive resources.
Operations Service/API availability; control-server uptime; MTTR Classic SRE measures for the classical stack and APIs.
Optimization Total QPU runtime (and shots) by PI/lab/project Governance, chargeback, and investment narrative.
Optimization Cost per completed experiment; demand by department/project ROI, hybrid cloud comparison, planning.
Workflow Transpilation time & transpilation failure rate Quantum-relevant, log-friendly, explains perceived delay.

Example guardrails cited in-source: failed logins <5/hour/user (contextual); job failure rate <5% weekly baseline; median wait <2h for “standard” jobs; control server >99.5% weekly; APIs >99.9% monthly; reservation efficiency >75%.

Tier 2 — add with quantum & facility telemetry

Theme Metric Notes
Device Calibration age at execution; gate fidelity & readout error trends Explain outcomes vs. stale calibration; trend vs. static thresholds.
Device T1/T2 trends; simulator vs. hardware divergence Requires domain interpretation and stable ingestion.
Facility Cryogenic temperature/pressure stability; power/vibration/RF correlation Unique QCS value; integrate BMS/sensors.
Workflow End-to-end latency (notebook → results); stage failure rates Requires workflow/correlation IDs across systems.
Integrity Metadata completeness; reproducibility reruns; audit response time Grant and publication defensibility.

Defer or use cautiously at first

  • Generic anomaly scores without calibration context
  • Static thresholds on noisy quantum signals
  • Predictive maintenance before enough history and labeled events
  • Cross-device benchmarks on heterogeneous stacks

Phased rollout (aligned across sources)

Phase Focus Examples
1 — Weeks 1–4 Foundation IAM & privileged monitoring; scheduler/queue dashboards; SIEM correlation; compliance/chargeback baselines; classical infra health.
2 — Months 2–3 Quantum-aware integration Calibration/device metrics; controller/firmware; facility/environment; hybrid workflow tracing; reproducibility metadata.
3 — Months 4–6 Advanced analytics Drift and aging trends; tuned ML on top of deterministic rules; predictive maintenance when data supports it.

Data ingestion layers (summary)

  1. Identity & access: IdP/MFA, VPN, bastion, SSH, notebooks, API gateways, vendor portals
  2. Classical & orchestration: Schedulers, OS/app logs, K8s, transpilers, middleware, storage
  3. Quantum control: Controllers, firmware, pulse/control interfaces, health events
  4. Facility: Cryogenics, HVAC, power, vibration, RF where available
  5. Governance: Project/PI tags, usage/billing exports, ticket/change records
