2. Most defensible MTTR-related metrics

The document supports strong claims about diagnosis and investigation, not an audited end-to-end MTTR without timestamps for remediation.

2a. Evidence-backed (high confidence)

Log-derived quantities (not model opinion):

  • Two disruption windows: 08:51–08:54 EDT and 09:09–09:12 EDT.
  • ~720 F5 “No members available” (or equivalent) events aligned with downstream symptoms.
  • 31 hosts in HOSTSTATE::DOWN (Icinga); 177 CRITICAL service checks (per ChatGPT section).
  • 230 pool-down / 231 pool-up transitions; Opus cites 199 and 254 status flaps across the two waves.
  • ~22 s cascade narrative from 08:51:18 EDT (Opus).
  • Post-check: major surge eased; residual flapping on some pools/lists.

2b. Investigation-time framing (estimates — label clearly)

  • With AI + Splunk: Probable proximate cause and cross-domain story in one interactive session; diagnostic leg in minutes vs. hours (no formal stopwatch in source).
  • Without comparable capability:
    • ChatGPT: 1–3+ hours for similar cross-domain confidence (staffing/handoff dependent).
    • Opus: 4–12 hours for full classic war-room handling of comparable complexity.

Defensible published band: “Tens of minutes under AI-assisted Splunk vs. ~1–4+ hours for equivalent multi-source confidence in typical enterprise conditions,” with upper tail 4–12 h documented as scenario-dependent.

Do not label the entire outcome as measured MTTR unless you add instrumentation (open/close, change records, verification tied to remediation).
