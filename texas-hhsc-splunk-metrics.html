Splunk as a Data Fabric for Texas HHS

Defensible metrics summary — reactive to proactive analytics

Synthesis of cross-model analyses (Gemini 3.1, GPT-4.5 Pro, Claude Haiku) on HHSC digital modernization.

Independent analyses agree that integrating Splunk into Texas Health and Human Services Commission (HHSC) infrastructure would move the agency from a reactive, manual, and siloed operating model toward a proactive, real-time analytics architecture. Splunk is positioned as a foundational data fabric that correlates disparate sources (for example TWC, IRS, SSA, TIERS, and EVV) to support fiscal risk management, fraud prevention, operational efficiency, and security compliance.

Strategic objectives (consensus)

  • Fiscal risk mitigation: Reduce exposure to federal penalties tied to Payment Error Rate (PER) thresholds and administrative cost-sharing.
  • Fraud, waste, and abuse (FWA): Shift from pay-and-chase audits toward real-time and predictive anomaly detection.
  • Operational efficiency: Automate manual reporting and aggregation to reclaim staff time for high-risk case management.
  • Security and compliance: Centralize audit logging and user behavior analytics to shorten mean time to detect (MTTD) and strengthen protections around PII/PHI.
